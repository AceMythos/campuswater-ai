# **1M1B AI for Sustainability Virtual Internship**

## **Project Creation & Guideline Document**

*(In Collaboration with IBM SkillsBuild & AICTE)*

## **1\. Project Context: How to Think About Your AI \+ Sustainability Project**

Before you begin building your project, it is important to pause and **frame your thinking correctly**.

This internship is not about building complex technology for the sake of it. It is about **learning how to observe real-world problems, think critically, and apply AI responsibly to create meaningful impact**.

As a student, you may wonder:

* What problem should I choose?  
* Does my idea need to be very big or advanced?  
* How do I connect AI with sustainability in a practical way?

This project is designed to help you answer those questions.

You are encouraged to start from **your surroundings**:

* Issues you notice in your campus, city, village, or community  
* Problems faced by people around you  
* Environmental, social, or resource-related challenges you care about

The goal is to **think like a problem-solver**, not just a technologist.

Your project should show:

* Clear thinking about the problem  
* Logical use of AI tools or workflows  
* Alignment with one or more Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)  
* Awareness of ethics, responsibility, and real-world impact

There is no single “right” idea. What matters most is **clarity of thought, relevance, and responsible application of AI**.

## **2\. Purpose of This Document**

This document is a step-by-step guide to help students **choose a sustainability problem, map it to an SDG, and design an AI-powered solution** as part of the 1M1B – IBM SkillsBuild AI \+ Sustainability Virtual Internship.

Students are encouraged to:

* Pick **any real-world problem** linked to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)  
* Apply **AI tools, models, or workflows** learned during the internship  
* Build a **conceptual or working prototype** that demonstrates problem-solving using AI

There are **no restrictions on domains** as long as the problem connects to sustainability and responsible AI.

## **2\. What Makes a Good AI \+ Sustainability Project**

A strong project should clearly answer four questions:

1. **What problem are you solving?** (SDG-aligned)  
2. **Who is affected by this problem?** (users, communities, environment)  
3. **Why is AI needed?** (prediction, automation, insight, scale)  
4. **How does your solution create impact?** (social, environmental, economic)

Projects may be:

* Awareness tools  
* Decision-support systems  
* Predictive or analytical models  
* Conversational AI or assistants  
* Monitoring or early-warning systems

## **3\. Choosing Your Problem Statement (SDG Mapping)**

### **Step 1: Select an SDG**

Choose **one primary SDG** your project will address.

Examples:

* SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being  
* SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation  
* SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy  
* SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities  
* SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production  
* SDG 13: Climate Action  
* SDG 15: Life on Land

You may reference **secondary SDGs**, but keep one main focus.

### **Step 2: Identify a Real Problem**

Ask yourself:

* Is this problem visible around me (campus, city, village, industry)?  
* Is data involved or generated?  
* Can technology improve speed, accuracy, access, or scale?

**Good Problem Statement Format:**

**How might we use AI to \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ so that \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ can become more sustainable?**

## **4\. Role of AI in Your Project**

Your project must clearly demonstrate **where and how AI is used**.

You may use AI for:

* Classification or prediction  
* Information retrieval and summarization  
* Pattern detection  
* Decision-making support  
* Automation using agents  
* Conversational interfaces

### **Allowed AI Components**

Students may use:

* Prompt engineering  
* IBM Granite Models  
* Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)  
* Agentic AI workflows  
* Entity extraction and summarization  
* Multimodal inputs (text, image, document)  
* Others

Projects **do not require advanced coding**. Logic, workflow design, and responsible use of AI are equally valued.

## **5\. Project Ideation Using Design Thinking**

### **Stage 1: Empathize**

* Who faces this problem?  
* What challenges do they experience?  
* Why does the problem persist?

### **Stage 2: Define**

Clearly write:

* Problem statement  
* Target users  
* Current gaps or inefficiencies

### **Stage 3: Ideate**

* Brainstorm AI-based solutions  
* Think of automation, prediction, or decision support  
* Keep feasibility in mind

### **Stage 4: Prototype**

* Flowchart or workflow diagram  
* Prompt logic or RAG pipeline  
* Sample inputs and outputs

### **Stage 5: Test & Refine**

* Mentor feedback  
* Improve clarity, ethics, and usefulness

## **7\. Responsible AI Guidelines (Mandatory)**

Every project must address:

* **Fairness:** Avoid bias in data or assumptions  
* **Transparency:** Explain how AI reaches outcomes  
* **Ethics:** No harmful, discriminatory, or misleading use  
* **Privacy:** Avoid personal or sensitive data misuse

Include a short section in your project:

Responsible AI Considerations

## **8\. Final Project Deliverables**

Each student must submit **PPT or PDF** with the following details:

1. **Project Description**  
   * Title  
   * Your Name/Names and College Names  
   * SDG Alignment  
   * Problem Statement  
   * AI Solution Overview  
   * Target Users  
   * Responsible AI Considerations  
   * Expected Impact  
2. **Prototype or Demo** (any one)  
   * Prompt workflows  
   * RAG demo  
   * Agent logic

   * What changes if this solution is implemented?  
   * Who benefits and how?

## **9\. Key Message to Students**

You are **not expected to solve the entire world’s problem**.

You are expected to:

* Think critically  
* Apply AI responsibly  
* Design with purpose  
* Build for impact

This project is your opportunity to demonstrate how **AI can be a force for sustainability, inclusion, and positive change**.
